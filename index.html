About Me

(Hey there! Just a heads up that this is a temporary page while my real website is under construction. Feel free to reach out via Twitter, or LinkedIn if you're curious about any of my current projects or want to learn more about anything listed here in the meantime! Thank you for your patience!)

I'm a recent graduate of Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology and a freshman Regents' and Chancellor's Scholar at the University of California, Berkeley. I'm also the founder and CEO of Everybody Code Now!, a national nonprofit working to expose more students to Computer Science. I like problem solving, public speaking, and puns (and alliteration).

Some classes I've taken/am currently taking include:

  • CS61A: Structure and Interpretation of Programs
  • AP Computer Science A Plus Data Structures
  • Mobile and Web Application Development
  • AP Calculus AB and AP Calculus BC
  • AP Spanish Language & Culture
  • Biology, Chemistry, and Physics

What I'm Up To Right Now

I was Class of 2017 at Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology, a Governor's STEM school in Alexandria, VA.

My coursework was focused on Computer Science, and still is now that I'm a college student. I'm fascinated by application development and cybersecurity and have taken classes like Mobile Application Development (taught using Android) and Web Application Development. My senior year, I worked in our Mobile and Application Development Laboratory for my senior research project.

I'm also passionate about community service, social entrepreneurship, and public speaking- especially when it relates to CS, youth, and STEM education! Please contact me if you are interested in working together!

Recent Events and Public Speaking

I speak on a variety of topics ranging from STEM education to Diversity in tech. Additionally, I give benefit dance performances for charity fundraisers and have participated in (and won) Model UN conferences both as a delegate and committee chair.

My most recent talk was at the US State Department Global Ties Conference.
Other recent talks include:
  • The White House Champions of Change Reunion in December 2015 under President Obama to an audience of over 400 people. I shared the stage with Megan Smith, then the US CTO and four other Champions of Change working to improve STEM education and research access for Americans.
  • The IgniteSTEM conference hosted at Columbia University on April 21st, 2016 to more than 150 New York area educators on how to bring CS education to schools.
  • I also gave the keynote talk at the AAUW STEMtastics conference for 500+ middle school girls and their families on March 2nd, 2016.

If you would like to collaborate at an upcoming event, please contact me! I would love to be involved :)

Everybody Code Now!, Founder & Executive Director

I was able to discover my passion for Computer Science thanks to TJ and an incredible group of mentors that encouraged me along the way. It was important to me that such opportunities existed for everyone from a young age.

That's why I founded Everybody Code Now! and serve as the Executive Director. So far, we have exposed more than 1000 students to Computer Science and helped schools raise more than $35,000 to fund future STEM activities in schools.

If you're interested in learning more or working together, please visit our website at everybodycodenow.org.

Ashburn Teen Leadership Council, Director

I served as the Director of the Ashburn Teen Leadership Council for five years, working with area youth and the Ashburn Library to improve teen engagement and encourage teens to do community service. I was elected/re-elected each year and served through August 2017.
More information about the Council can be found at ashburntlc.weebly.com.

Awards & Public Speaking

2015 White House Champion of Change

1 of 11 White House Young Women Empowering Communities Champions of Change. Was invited to the White House for this accomplishment in September 2015 and able to speak with top officials such as US CTO Megan Smith and Senior Advisor Valerie Jarrett.

2-Time Carson Scholar

Carson scholarship honors teens who demonstrate humanitarian qualities and have outstanding academic achievement. Won the scholarship in 2015 and was again honored as a recognized scholar in 2016.

NCWIT Aspirations in Computing Award

Awarded to high school girls for their academic achievement and technical aspirations. Received the Virginia/DC Runner-Up Award in 2015 and a 2016 Certificate of Distinction

2 Time Stanford University She++ #include Fellow

One of 30 students invited to Stanford Universitys 2015 #include Summit. Toured major tech companies and was able to hear from tech leaders like Sheryl Sandberg. Chosen as a 2016 Fellow in recognition of work to promote CS Education work by founding Everybody Code Now!

Google & Center for American Progress "Future of Innovation" Panel

Hosted at Google DC in February 2016, I spoke on a panel alongside Code.org's COO and executives from the National Center for Women and Information Technology and the Kapor Center. Check out the interview I did with Education Week on the event!

Virginia Governor's Diwali Dinner

I spoke at the Diwali Dinner celebration for Virginia Governor Terry McAuliffe in December 2015 on the importance of Computer Science education for youth.

2016 IgniteSTEM Conference

I spoke at IgniteSTEM, hosted at Columbia University, in April 2016. I spoke on the importance of CS education, and how to integrate the subject into classrooms, to more than 150 New York area educators

AAUW STEMtastics Conference 2016

I was the Keynote Speaker at AAUW's STEMtastics conference in March 2016, where I spoke to the families of more than 300 middle school girls about STEM education and diversity. Check out the talk video here!

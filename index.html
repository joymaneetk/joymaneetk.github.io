My Interests

My high school didn't have a strong computer science program and only offered its first computer science class when I was a junior. However, I feel fortunate to have discovered this field at all, because I quickly fell in love with it for its vast applications and ability to help people on a large scale.

I joined the robotics team to further explore computer science and became captain in senior year. I am facinated by robotics, web and mobile application development, cybersecurity, and data science. In senior year, for my mathematics research assesment, I used data science through algorithms and processes to understand and interpret data from my research on the SIR model for the spread of diseases. I coded in Java to create an algorithm to model the Euler's method linear approximation of the spread of diseaes and modeled the data to compare it to data from the Runge Kutta algorithm.

I'm also passionate about community service, social justice and activism, and public speaking, specifically to encourage underpriviledged youth to pursue STEM education.

Spreading the Language of Code

I was eager to share my passion for Computer Science and started the Coding club at school which aimed to give students access to the resources to make them comfortable with coding. I developed lessons each week for beginner level and more advanced level students to make sure everyone could be included, no matter their level of skill. I created this platform as a way to pave opportunities for everyone to get involved in coding from a young age.

Internationally, I was able to collaborate with the non-profit Yuvsatta and work with underprivileged girls in marginalized communities to teach them the importance of education, the need for diversity in STEM, and how to seek out resources to bridge the gender and wage gap.

Quarry Lane and Dublin City Ambassador
